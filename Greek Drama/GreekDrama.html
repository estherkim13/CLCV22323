Let's talk about Greek drama.

Aeschylus

The Persians, 472 BC.

- Set in Susa, the Persian Empire.
- First dream sequence narration in European theater
- Unusually early entry of the chorus in beginning
- Main character is Queen Atossa, wife of King Darius, mother of Xerxes
- Summoning of a ghost (King Darius) who condemns Xerxes' hubris
- Battle of Salamis where the Persians lost bitterly is described graphically
- Rebukes Xerxes decision to build a bridge over the Hellespont to expedite army
- Prophecy of another Persian defeat at the Battle of Plataea
- A king reeling from Persia's defeat, not death itself
- The pyschological aftermath of a battle lost

Seven Against Thebes, 467 BC.

- Third play in an Oedipus-themed trilogy (sometimes called Oedipodia; first and second plays were called Laius and Oedipus) produced by Aeschylus
- Concerns battle between Argives (led by seven champions incl. Polynices) and the army of Thebes led by Eteocles
- Takes place after the self-inflicted blinding of Oedipus, and his two sons, Eteocles and Polynices had agreed to rule Thebes alternating by year
- After his first year as king, Eteocles refused to acquiese his powers to Polynices, causing Polynices to gather the Seven captains Against Thebes
- Most of the play is dialogue between Theban citizens and Eteocles as king, regarding the threat of the Seven
- Lengthy descriptions of the Seven are given as they go against each of the seven gates of Thebes
- Commander against the seventh gate is Polynices, Eteocles faces off with him one on one outside the gate
- Both brothers are killed in the battle
- Eteocles' and Polynices' dead bodies are brought onstage and mourned by the chorus
- Attacker
